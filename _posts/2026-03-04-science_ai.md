Ones everyone with access to the these systems can let them essentially write *correct* papers autonomously, the value of a paper as a scientific contribution has vanished. Unfortunately, the current system of academia is based on using papers as a benchmark of the quality of scientists.
What journals they published in, how many citations these papers have, what grants the person got - these benchmarks are what is used to judge scientists for employment.
However, these are terrible measures.
Peer review and therefore the journal a paper appeared in is often a farse, since the referees have no real incentive to invest a lot of time to review a paper.
More importantly, in an age where essentially all papers are first submitted as preprints, no-one cares if a journal accepted the paper, because the ideas exist in the preprint and everyone can use them, independently of the subjective opinion of two or three arbitrary people.
What matters is, if the individual scientists who use the content of a paper for their own work, themselves have understood the content and believe its correctness. 
Using peer review as a stamp of approval for an idea only encourages laziness and scientific disingenuity.
If you want to use and idea, because you believe in it, make sure that you have good reason to do so.

The journal system primarily exists for university administrations to have a reason, why to higher a person. 
I do not believe that "this makes decision for the university admin making easier" is a good use of taxpayers money.

Because of this disdain for the journal system, we need to reformulate how science works and build frameworks in which we can collaborate on the problems we deem important. 
Science is a collaborative effort that encompasses people all around the world and I think that solving problems, not publishing papers should become the main objective.
Because of this, I think that we will have to construct a structure in which we can constructively work together to solve the problems that exist. I do not think that every problem has to be applied, on the contrary, I think that being able to do research for the pure beauty of the subject has been a tremendously fruitful and beautiful force driving a large portion of the most impactful work.
